River Sampling

Volunteers regulary sample the Avon and its tributaries for the following:
  • Water quality checks (phosphates, nitrates, ammonia, conductivity)
  • Weekly water bacteria checks (e-coli, intestinal enterocci, culiforms)

Water quality checks

The map below shows sampling results taken by volunteers of the Avonvale River Action Group, Safe Avon, Angling Trust, and other concerned groups in the area. The graphs below are then generated from an export of that data with an expectation to update them here monthly.

This data should not be used to determine river safety but instead viewed as an indicator of general trends in river health.

Three key pollutants are displayed when you click on a location, along with a "good" threshold for comparison. These dashed horizontal line thresholds are derived from a variety of sources and are for indicative purposes only.

  • Conductivity measurements reflect the amount of dissolved salts and minerals in the river, with higher levels often indicating pollution from urban runoff, industrial discharge, or agricultural activities, which can impact water quality and aquatic life.
  • Phosphates often stem from fertilizers, detergents, and wastewater, and elevated levels contribute to harmful algal blooms, disrupting the ecosystem and water quality.
  • Nitrates in rivers primarily come from agricultural runoff and sewage, and high levels can lead to excessive algae growth, depleting oxygen and harming aquatic life.
  • Ammonia often indicates pollution from agricultural runoff, sewage, or industrial waste, signaling poor water quality and potential harm to aquatic life. Elevated ammonia levels can pose health risks to humans.

For reasons of graph visibility, conductivity readings are clipped at 3000
